# Brand Integration Rules

Read this file when the user specifies a brand for the blog post. If no brand is specified, omit the link and brand mention entirely.

---

## General Rules (Any Brand)

- Insert **one** natural hyperlink to the brand's official website — not a product page, the domain root or most relevant landing page
- Place the link in the **second half** of the article only — never in the opening or hook
- The brand must feel like a **natural discovery**, not an advertisement
- Immerse the reader in the problem or topic fully before the brand appears
- The brand name appears **no more than twice** in the full article (once as the linked mention, once optional in the conclusion)
- Never position the brand as the only solution — the article must stand on its own editorial merit
- Never use the brand mention as the article's "big reveal" — the insight must precede the mention

## Das Experten — Brand Integration Guide

**Philosophy:** Performance-first, evidence-driven, clinically intelligent oral care. Every ingredient has a job. No fluff.

**Voice in mentions:** Authoritative. Clinical. Direct. Never: "high quality", "natural wellness", vague claims.

**Positioning:** German-founded science brand. Not a pharmacy product — a specialist tool.

### Prohibited in Any Das Experten Mention

- ❌ "High quality" without supporting data
- ❌ Fear-based messaging ("your teeth are rotting")
- ❌ Comparing charcoal to fluoride (exception: DETOX clove remineralization data is valid)
- ❌ Natural-wellness vocabulary without scientific support ("gentle on nature")
- ❌ Claiming the product cures or treats disease (use: "supports," "helps reduce," "clinically shown to")
- ❌ Placing the brand in the first half of the article
- ❌ More than one hyperlink per article

---

### Tone of Brand Mention

Write the brand mention the way a journalist would reference a specialist tool — matter-of-factly, with one credible detail attached.

**Good example:**
> For cases where conventional pastes fall short, formulations built on enzyme cascades rather than abrasives — such as those developed by Das Experten — have shown measurable results in clinical settings without compromising enamel integrity.

**Bad example:**
> Das Experten makes amazing high-quality toothpastes that you should definitely try! [das-experten.com]
